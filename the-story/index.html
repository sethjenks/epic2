Privacy

We take patient privacy very seriously.

You control your data.

You opt-in, choose when and how to share your data, and can discontinue use at any time.

All shared data is aggregated and anonymized.

We only collect data we need to paint a rough texture of your behavior. We respect the privacy of your communication and activity.

You get value for sharing.

With your permission, data can be shared with your clinicians and care team to advance medical research and improve your care.

Data sharing is key for getting timely help for distressed users, but data sharing must be secure and should maintain a user's privacy and control of information to be shared. Ginger.io is built on a commitment to patients and the new deal for data. The team has built their platform to ensure respect for data ownership and privacy.

Professor Alex (Sandy) PentlandChief Privacy Advocate at Ginger.io, and Director of Human Dynamics Research at the MIT Media Lab

Suggested Reading

  • Consumer Data Privacy in a Networked World

    The White House's privacy framework, designed to protect consumers, embraces commonly recognized privacy principles and adapts them for the data-driven digital world.

  • Personal Data: The Emergence of a New Asset Class

    The World Economic Forum's treatise on personal data, its importance as a critical source of innovation, and the need for its protection.

  • EFF's Mobile User Privacy Bill of Rights

    The Electronic Frontier Foundation's discourse on the privacy rights of a mobile user and its guidelines for the best technical practices to preserve user privacy.

  • ID3

    ID3 is a new non-profit organization of multidisciplinary thinkers that's defining frameworks for preserving user privacy and control while sharing personal data.
